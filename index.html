Introduction

This project uses an Arduino Uno, a MQ-135 Gas Sensor, a DHT11 Temperature and Humidity Sensor, a Liquid Crystal Display, and two Potentiometers to monitor environmental conditions such as air quality, humidity, and detect gas and smoke.

Circuit Diagram

The gas sensor measures air quality; the temperature and humidity measures temperature and humidity; the Arduino activates LCD; the potentiometer adjusts the displayed items.

Environmental Impact Assessment

  • Materials: The MQ-135 gas sensor, DHT11 temperature and humidity sensor, potentiometer, and Arduino Uno are well-known electronic components manufactured according to modern standards. Although sourced from different suppliers, most of these components are RoHS-compliant, meaning they limit the use of hazardous substances and help reduce toxic electronic waste over time.
  • Energy Efficiency: The environmental monitoring system remains highly energy-efficient, even with components from multiple sources. The circuit operates at only 5V and consumes approximately 50–60 mA during use. Both the DHT11 and MQ-135 sensors are designed for low-power embedded systems, making them suitable for continuous monitoring with minimal energy demand.
  • Reusability: All components selected for this project are modular and reusable, regardless of their source. The Arduino Uno, sensors, and wiring can easily be removed and integrated into future projects. This greatly reduces electronic waste and extends the lifecycle of each component.
  • Packaging & Shipping: Since the components are obtained from different suppliers, the total packaging waste and transportation footprint are larger than if all items were purchased from a single provider. Each shipment contributes to increased carbon emissions and additional packaging material. However, most small electronic components come with minimal protective packaging, which helps mitigate the environmental impact.

Conclusion

This system effectively demonstrates the use of Arduino for air quality, humidity, and gas alert monitoring. It is cost-effective, educational, and sustainable due to its use of reusable components.
